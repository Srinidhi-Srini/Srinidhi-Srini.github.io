A unit cell is the most basic repeating part of a crystal structure. This is a concept I just learned in my MSE 104 class, so I thought I would reproduce it below and try to explain the different ones. 



## Simple Cubic Crystal Structure
The center of the atom is located at each corner of the cube. Since there are 8 corners, each corner contains 1/8 of an atom. Another way of visualizing this, is thinking each corner having a cube connected to its side, front and top. This in total is 1/8 as (1/2 x 1/2 x 1/2).

This structure is quite rare as it has a low packing density and only Polonium has this structure.
