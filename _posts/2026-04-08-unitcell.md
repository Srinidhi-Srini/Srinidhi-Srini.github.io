This is a concept I just learned in my MSE 104 class, so I thought I would reproduce it below and try to explain the different unit cells.  

A unit cell is the most basic repeating part of a crystal structure. Let's say there is a skyscraper and there are about 10 floors that are identical. Each floor is a unit cell, as it's just the most basic repeating part of the structure. 



## Simple Cubic Crystal Structure
The center of the atom is located at each corner of the cube. Since there are 8 corners, each corner contains 1/8 of an atom, so there is 1 atom in this structure. Another way of visualizing this, is thinking each corner having a cube connected to its side, front and top. This in total is 1/8 as (1/2 x 1/2 x 1/2).

{: style="text-align: center;"}

This structure is quite rare as it has a low packing density and only Polonium has this structure. 

## Body-Centered Cubic Structure
Like the Simple Cubic, the center of the atom is located at each corner of the cube. The only difference is that there is also a single atom in the center. There are 2 atoms/unit cell as 1 + 1/8 x 8 = 2. 

![SC](/assets/images/bp7.png){: style="display: block; margin: 0 auto;"}
{: style="text-align: center;"}

Some elements with this structure are Chromium, Iron and Molybedenum. 

## Face-Centered Cubic Structure
This one too has the atoms' centers in all the corners, in addition to 1/2 an atom on each face of the cube. There are 4 atoms/unit cell as 1/2 x 6 + 1/8 x 8 = 4. This one has the maximum Atomic Packing Factor (volume of atoms in unit cell / volume of unit cell) of 75%. Simple Cubic has an APF of 52% and Body-Centered Cubic has an APF of 68%. The percentage is what volume of the unit cell is filled with atoms.
