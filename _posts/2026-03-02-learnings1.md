## Chemistry (CHEM 20B)
This week, I had my 2nd chem midterm on Friday (2/27). It covered concepts from entropy to acid-base calculations. Below is a summary from my Midterm 2 concepts [quizlet]((https://quizlet.com/1143781839/chem-20b-midterm-2-flash-cards/)). 

### Entropy: possibilities of position
* $$\Delta$$S > 0: more possibilites (spontaneous)
* $$\Delta$$S < 0: less possibilities

### Gibbs Free Energy: reversible work done at constant temperature and pressure
* $$\Delta$$G = $$\Delta$$H - T $$\Delta$$S
* $$\Delta$$G > 0, $$\Delta$$S < 0(not spontaneous)
* $$\Delta$$G < 0, $$\Delta$$S > 0 (spontanous)
* $$\Delta$$G = 0 (reversible)

### Enthalpy vs Entropy Driven
* $$\Delta$$S > 0, entropy driven & spontaneous @ high T
* $$\Delta$$H < 0, enthalphy driven & spontaneous @ low T
* T* = $$\frac{\Delta H}{\Delta S}$$

### Relationship of $$\Delta$$G and K (equilibrium constant)
* $$\Delta$$G > 0, K < 0(not spontaneous)
* $$\Delta$$G < 0, K > 0 (spontanous)

### Reaction Quotient Q
* Q < K: shifts right, reactants turn into products (endothermic)
* Q > K: shifts left, products turn into reactants (exothermic)

### Acid-Base
* Acid: accepts lone pair, H donator
* Base: donates lone pair, H acceptor
* Conjugate Acid: Base after accepting H
* Conjugate Base: Acid after donating H
* HA + H2O -> A + H3O+
* B + H2O -> HB + OH-

### Reactions with Acid-Base
* strong acid + strong base: H3O+ + OH- -> 2H20
* strong acid + weak base: H3O+ + B -> HB + H2O
* weak acid + strong base: HA + OH- -> A + H2O
